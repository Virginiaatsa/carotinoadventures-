Pakistan —
The Rooftop of the World

Karakoram Highway. Hunza Valley. Skardu. Lahore's street food. This is my pilot trip — and I'm documenting the full route so you can do it too.

3–4 Weeks Route Focused Karakoram Range Backpacker Friendly Instagram Gold

Why Pakistan?

Pakistan is one of the most jaw-dropping, off-the-beaten-path destinations on earth. The Karakoram Highway, dubbed the "Eighth Wonder of the World," snakes through mountains that make the Alps look like hills. And the hospitality? Pakistani hospitality is legendary.

Yet most people don't go. They've been misled by outdated travel warnings and media narratives. Tourist regions like Gilgit-Baltistan and Hunza Valley have an incredibly low crime rate and locals who will quite literally invite you into their homes for chai.

From Lahore's architecture to the north's glacier-fed valleys, the variety of landscapes in one trip is incredible.

Concept check: Pakistan combines huge mountain scenery, rich city culture, and warm local hospitality in one unforgettable route.

The Route

This is the route I'm planning for the pilot trip. It covers the greatest highlights through a logical south-to-north flow.

🇮🇵 Week 1 — Lahore: The Gateway

Days 1–7
1–2
Arrival & Lahore Old City

Land in Lahore, get settled in a hostel. Explore the Walled City, Lahore Fort, and Badshahi Mosque on foot.

3–4
Food Tour & Anarkali Bazaar

Street food deep dive — nihari, biryani, lassi. Anarkali Bazaar for authentic Pakistani market culture.

5–7
Lahore to Islamabad

Day trip or short bus to Islamabad. Faisal Mosque, the Margalla Hills hike, and a calm base before heading north.

🏔 Week 2 — Karakoram Highway

Days 8–14
8–9
Islamabad to Gilgit

NATCO bus up the KKH — a full day of jaw-dropping mountain scenery. One of the world's great road journeys.

10–11
Gilgit Base & Rakaposhi Views

Acclimatise in Gilgit. Day hike with views of Rakaposhi (7,788m). Gear check for the trek ahead.

12–14
Hunza Valley & Attabad Lake

The crown jewel. Turquoise lake, apricot orchards, ancient Baltit and Altit forts. Sunrise from Eagle's Nest viewpoint.

⛰ Week 3–4 — Skardu & Deosai

Days 15–25
15–16
Hunza to Skardu

Jeep transfer or local bus to Skardu — an iconic mountain hub and gateway to Deosai National Park.

17–18
Deosai Plateau

The world's second highest plateau at 4,114m. Wildflowers, Himalayan brown bears, and zero crowds.

19–25
Shigar Valley & Upper Kachura

Explore local villages, alpine lakes, and broad mountain views with day hikes and jeep routes from Skardu.

Practical Info

  • Visa: Pakistan eVisa is available online for most nationalities. Takes 2–5 business days. Apply at least 2 weeks before travel.
  • Safety: Tourist areas (Gilgit-Baltistan, Hunza, Lahore, Islamabad) are safe. Always check your government's current travel advice and register with your embassy.
  • Currency: Pakistani Rupee (PKR). ATMs available in Lahore, Islamabad, Gilgit. In remote areas, carry cash.
  • SIM card: Buy a SIM at Lahore airport. Jazz or Zong are best for data coverage in the north.
  • Best time to visit: May–October for the Karakoram and northern valleys.
  • Altitude: Gilgit is at ~1,500m and Skardu at ~2,228m. Take time to acclimatise before long hikes.
  • Language: Urdu is official. English is widely spoken in cities and tourist areas. Learning "shukriya" (thank you) goes a long way.
  • Dress code: Conservative dress is respectful. Shoulders and knees covered in cities. In the mountains, trekking gear is fine.
